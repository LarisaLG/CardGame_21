HOW TO PLAY BLACKJACK

OBJECTIVE: The Blackjack's game objective is to get a hand total scores of closer to 21 than the dealer without going over 21 (busting).

CARD VALUES: Kings, queens, and jacks are worth 10 points. Numbered cards retain their face value. Aces are worth either 1 point or 11 points depending on what value advantages the player.

HOW TO PLAY: If the dealer does not deal himself an ace as a face-up card, then player is asked if he/she would like to “hit” or “stand”. To hit is to ask for another card, to stand is to pass. If you choose to hit and then receive a card that puts you over the value of 21, you’ve busted. You may continue to hit until you are satisfied with your hand.

A starting hand of a 10 valued card and an Ace is called a Blackjack and beats all hands other than another Blackjack.

If both the player and dealer have Blackjack, the result is tie.
